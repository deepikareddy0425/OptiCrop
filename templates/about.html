About the project

Smart Agricultural Production Optimization Engine

OptiCrop is a machine-learning powered web application that recommends the most suitable crop for a given set of soil and environmental conditions. It's built to help farmers, agricultural researchers, and agribusinesses make faster, data-driven planting decisions.

The seven signals it reads

N — Nitrogen P — Phosphorous K — Potassium Temperature Humidity pH Rainfall

How a recommendation is produced

1. Collect

Soil test readings and local climate data are gathered from the field or a weather source.

2. Predict

A trained classifier (chosen by comparing Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and KNN) scores every candidate crop.

3. Recommend

The highest-confidence crop is returned, along with a short note on why it fits those conditions.

Under the hood

Data preprocessing includes null checks, IQR-based outlier handling, and feature scaling. K-Means clustering is used exploratively to surface natural groupings in growing conditions before supervised models are trained and evaluated on Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-score. The best-performing model is serialized and served through this Flask application.
